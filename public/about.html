About Me

Richard Osei Bofah is a Development Economist working with the National Development Planning Commission (NDPC) as Chief Analyst.
He is the SDGs National Coordinator with the mandate to coordinate the implementation of the SDGs and AU Agenda 2063.
He coordinated the preparation of the country’s 2022 Voluntary National Review of the Sustainable Development Goals and participated in the 2022 High-Level Political Forum (HLPF) in New York.
He also coordinates the reporting on AU Agenda 2063.

Richard represented Ghana at the Eighth Africa Regional Forum on Sustainable Development (8th ARFSD), where he highlighted Ghana’s progress on global and continental goals in Kigali, Rwanda, 2022.
He is the Technical Lead for Ghana’s Human Capital Development Strategy and Future of Work Project.
He has also been instrumental in the preparation of other key national documents including: Medium-Term National Development Policy Framework for 2022-2025, Ghana@100, National Annual Progress Reports, Development Monitors, District COVID-19 Recovery Plans, etc.

Prior to working with Commission, Richard worked as a Research Analyst with the New York University. He also worked as a Principal Research Assistant with the Institute of Statistical, Social and Economic Research (ISSER) of the University of Ghana on a 3-year project funded by the International Development Research Centre (IDRC) entitled “Gender and Enterprise Development in Africa: A cross-country comparative study” for Ghana, Kenya and Uganda.
He also worked as a Graduate Teaching Assistant with the Department of Economics, University of Ghana.

Richard holds a Doctor of Philosophy degree in Development Economics, a Master of Philosophy Degree in Economics and a Bachelor of Arts Degree in Economics with Mathematics, from the University of Ghana.
He also holds an industry Masters Programme in Data Science with multiple skills and certificates in the dynamic fields of artificial intelligence, machine learning and deep learning, etc.
He was a visiting Doctoral Fellow with the United Nations University World Institute for Development Economics Research (UNU-WIDER), Helsinki, Finland, 2016.
